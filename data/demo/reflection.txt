Self-reflection in adaptive RAG acts as a hallucination check. After the model produces a candidate answer, a verifier examines whether the claims are actually grounded in the retrieved evidence. The verifier can label the result as grounded, partially grounded, or hallucinated.

If the answer is weakly supported, the system can trigger additional retrieval, revise the response, or explicitly decline to answer. This makes adaptive RAG more robust than a pipeline that retrieves once and answers regardless of evidence quality.
